What the market usually wants:
• Highest demand: 3/2 and 4/2 layouts, functional kitchens, updated primary bath, open living areas.
• Typical flip sweet spot: 1,200-2,400 sqft in neighborhoods with strong school/commuter demand.
• Avoid odd layouts, severe external obsolescence, and homes with no nearby comp support.

Deal Rules (Use These Defaults)

Minimum Net Profit: $30,000 minimum (better target: $40,000+).

Minimum ARV Margin: 12% minimum net margin.

ARV margin means: (Net Profit ÷ ARV). Example: $36k profit on $300k ARV = 12% margin.

Max Hold Time Target: 90 days total (buy + rehab + sale).

Hard Pass Conditions (be strict)

  • Projected net profit is below your minimum threshold after all costs.
  • Foundation movement or structural issues without enough discount.
  • Flood-zone insurance/mitigation costs make the deal unworkable.
  • ARV relies on outlier comps instead of solid nearby sold data.
  • Estimated rehab + holding costs push projected net below minimum target.
  • Layout/location issues likely to cause long DOM after rehab.

Worksheet 4 — Financing + Insurance Prep

Goal: Lock funding and coverage before you’re under pressure.

Financing checklist (critical):
• Ask if interest is charged on full approved loan or only disbursed draws (big cost difference).
• Ask draw timing: weekly, biweekly, or milestone-only. Slow draws can stall subs and extend hold time.
• Confirm inspection fee per draw, minimum interest months, and extension costs.
• Prefer lenders with clear draw turnaround time (example: funds released in 48-72 hrs after inspection).

Insurance: What to Ask For (Practical)

  • Builder’s Risk (or Vacant Dwelling) policy for renovation period.
  • General Liability at least $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate.
  • Dwelling/replacement coverage near full rebuild cost (ask agent for replacement-cost estimate).
  • If needed for lender: lender listed as mortgagee + additional insured requirements confirmed.
  • Add flood/wind/hail endorsements if property risk requires it (don’t assume included).
  • Coverage start date must be at/just before closing, not after contractor start.
